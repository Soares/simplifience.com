How to use this site

Welcome to simplifience.

If you get lost, click on the title of the article or the big simplifience logo at the bottom of the page. It will take you back to simplifience.com.

Articles are grouped in sequences. You can navigate a sequence using the links at the bottom of a page. Sometimes one article appears in multiple sequences: keep track of which sequence you’re reading when you navigate. Once you reach the end of a sequence you’ll see a bunch of other sequences at the bottom of the page.

Within each article, there are side commentaries, which expand on colored text. Teal is the standard simplifience color. Other colors have different meanings:

Commentary boxes look like this.

Blue for discussing the rules of a system and pointing to math resources.

Navy blue for pedantic clarifications.

Purple as a secondary voice, often antithetical.

Magenta for discussing experiments and pointing to scientific resources.

Red for ranting about mistakes made by the mainstream.

Orange for content that is not yet completed.

Yellow to caution you about common misinterpretations.

Green to define new terminology.

Don’t memorize these. Don’t memorize anything. The meaning will be obvious in context.

Some articles will take on one of these colors entirely. For example, articles on the nature of science take on the magenta color. Such articles are introductions to different subjects such as science and mathematics. They’re useful articles, but all discussions of the workings of reality will occur in the teal articles.

Articles will have many links in them. Links will be blue if you haven’t visited the linked page and purple if you have. You’ll also see some orange links. These link to stubs of articles that are planned but not yet completed.
